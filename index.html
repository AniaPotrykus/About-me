  • Digital Humanities Project | 2024

    The projects aimed to explore how the social networks of literary characters vary across different languages. The findings revealed that the number of characters and their relationships are not isomorphic, shifting with the language due to both linguistic characteristics and translators' choices. This research enhanced the methodology of literary studies by showcasing how NLP tools can effectively support humanities analyses. Achieving these results required the use of Python and specialized libraries such as NLTK and SpaCy.
